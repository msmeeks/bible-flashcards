ESV Lookup & Attribution

Looking up a verse from Crossway's ESV Bible API, with the consent dialog and copyright footer that come with it.

Video capture pending. Storyboard below describes the flow until a real recording is captured.
1

Choose the ESV chip

On Add Verse, the ESV chip — labeled "ESV · Personal use · 500-verse cap" — is tapped below the BSB/KJV/WEB segmented control.

Add Verse → [ESV · Personal use · 500-verse cap]
2

First-time consent

Typing a reference and tapping "Look up verse" for the first time shows a consent dialog naming api.esv.org directly and disclosing the 500-verse cap, separate from any other lookup source's consent.

"This sends the reference to api.esv.org (Crossway). Up to 500 ESV verses may be stored. Continue?"
3

Preview with attribution

Once the verse text returns, a preview appears. Saving opens a confirmation dialog that includes the collapsible ESV copyright footer.

Romans 8:28 (ESV) — "And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good..."
ESV® · tap to expand full copyright notice
4

Persistent attribution everywhere

The same collapsible footer reappears on Verse Detail, Test, and Review screens whenever an ESV verse is shown — its collapsed/expanded state is remembered across all of them.

Verse Detail → ESV® footer (state remembered from Add Verse)
